Introduction¶

This project looks at the sales of Chemicals, Coal, Petrol and Vehicles in the US between 1971 and 1991. The data from 252 rows with each divided up into 12 months.

Website https://datamarket.com/data/set/22nd/us-monthly-sales-of-petroleum-and-related-products-jan-1971-dec-1991#!ds=22nd!2ekn&display=line

df['year'] = pd.DatetimeIndex(df['Month']).year

Each plot shows the general trend for Chemicals, Coal, Petrol and Vehicles sales. Each X label represents 2.5 years from 1971 to 1991. The vertical lines represent the stability of each point, the larger the vertical line the more unstable the sales were at that time.

In [6]:
plt.figure(figsize=(20,10))
plt.style.available
plt.plot(df.year, df.Chemicals, color='blue')
plt.xticks(rotation=90)
plt.title('Chemical Sales Between 1971 and 1991', fontsize=20)
plt.xlabel('Year', fontsize=15)
plt.ylabel('Sales', fontsize=15)
plt.xlim(1970, 1992)
plt.style.use('ggplot')
plt.show()

Chemical sales in the US show a gradual increase over this time period. The vertical line shows the sales over this time remained relitvilty stable, with sales raising from under 5 to just under 25 in the 20 year period.

Petrol sales remained stable up to 1978, there was a significant sales increase from 1979 to 1981, Although, the sales started to decrease with the Iraq - Iran war starting in September in 1980. Also, the sales increase in 1990 may have been a result of Iraq invasion of Kuwait in 1990.
